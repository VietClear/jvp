Tone Guide

Because in Vietnamese, tone is meaning.

In Vietnamese, even if your pronunciation is otherwise perfect, you will still be misunderstood if you get the tone wrong.

  • ma (ghost) — ngang →
  • má (mother) — sắc ↗
  • mà (but) — huyền ↘
  • mả (grave) — hỏi ↘↗
  • mạ (rice seedling) — nặng ↘●

There are six tones in Vietnamese, but in the South only five are used. VietClear teaches the Southern tones — don’t worry, you’ll still be understood everywhere in the country.

ngang sắc huyền hỏi nặng
ngang (level) — → steady, flat pitch
sắc (rising) — ↗ short, sharp rise
huyền (falling) — ↘ smooth downward fall
hỏi (dipping / fall-rise) — ↘↗ fall then rise
nặng (heavy / low fall with stop) — ↘● abrupt stop
